DN1000 High-Performance Plastic Coated Composite Steel Pipe

The Main Artery for Urban and Industrial Water Transportation Systems

Product Core Value and Industry Positioning

As a core pipeline for large-scale urban and industrial water transportation systems, DN1000 plastic-coated steel pipe perfectly combines the mechanical strength of steel materials with the excellent corrosion resistance of plastic coatings. Compared to traditional steel pipes, the smoothness of the inner wall has improved by more than 40%, and transportation efficiency has increased by 15% to 20%, making it an ideal choice for long-distance, high-flow projects such as inter-regional water transportation and urban main water supply networks.

Innovative Process and Material Breakthroughs

Dual Coating Protection System

  • Inner Coating: Food-grade epoxy resin (thickness ≥0.35mm), compliant with GB/T 17219 drinking water hygiene standards.
  • Outer Coating: High-density PE or epoxy powder (thickness ≥0.5mm), resistant to UV and chemical corrosion, accommodating temperature variations from -40°C to 80°C.

Structural Reinforcement Design

  • High-Strength Base Pipe: Q235B/Q355C spiral welded steel pipe (9-10mm), with maximum pressure resistance of 2.5MPa and strong deformation resistance.
  • Process Innovation: "Pipe end expansion + chamfering" process protects the coating at connection points, with peel strength >50N/cm.

Selection Technical Guidance

$ Q(m^3/h) = v(m/s) \times S(m^2) \times 3600 $

Pressure Matching Principles

  • PN10 Class (1.0MPa): Suitable for general urban water supply.
  • PN16 Class (1.6MPa): Suitable for mountainous or hilly systems requiring pump pressurization.
